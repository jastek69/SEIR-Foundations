# SEIR-Foundations
Systems Engineering and Infrastructure Integration

Applied Cloud & Platform Engineering

A 2-Year Applied Program in Cloud, Automation, and Operational Judgment

## What This Program Is

Applied Cloud & Platform Engineering is a 2-year, hands-on program designed to teach how real computing systems are built, automated, secured, and operated in the real world. This is not a bootcamp or a certification class. The program focuses on long-term skill development, practical responsibility, and learning how to think clearly when systems fail.

Students work with real infrastructure, real constraints, and real consequences. Throughout the program they build cloud environments, automate deployments, troubleshoot failures, manage access controls, and develop the judgment required to operate production systems responsibly.

The objective is not simply to pass exams or learn tools. The objective is to develop professionals who can understand complex systems, identify risk, and make sound decisions under pressure.

## How This Program Is Taught

Most programs teach technologies in isolation and assume everything works as expected. This program takes a different approach.

Students learn how systems behave when things do not go according to plan. They practice reading infrastructure plans, interpreting logs, debugging failures, recovering from outages, and making careful decisions when information is incomplete.

Difficulty is intentional, structured, and supported. Students are expected to encounter failure, uncertainty, and ambiguity because those conditions exist in every real engineering environment. The goal is not speed or memorization, but competence, resilience, and operational confidence.

Labs are designed to simulate realistic engineering scenarios involving cloud infrastructure, networking, identity management, automation, security, platform operations, and AI-assisted workflows.

## Skills and Focus Areas

Students develop practical experience in:

* Cloud Infrastructure Engineering
* Infrastructure as Code (Terraform)
* Platform Engineering
* CI/CD and Deployment Pipelines
* GitOps and Change Management
* Cloud Networking and Connectivity
* Identity and Access Management
* System Reliability and Operations
* Security Fundamentals and Risk Management
* AI-Assisted Engineering Workflows

Students learn to automate systems using both Bash and Python, beginning with foundational scripting and progressing toward automation workflows used in modern cloud environments. Scripts are used to provision resources, process data, troubleshoot systems, validate deployments, and support operational tasks.

Git and GitOps practices are integrated throughout the program. Students learn how infrastructure and application changes are managed through version control, pull requests, peer review, and automated deployment pipelines. The emphasis is on creating predictable, auditable, and repeatable engineering processes rather than relying on manual changes.

Modern AI tools are introduced as engineering assistants rather than replacements for human judgment. Students work with Claude Sonnet through Amazon Bedrock to perform infrastructure analysis, incident investigation, report generation, documentation support, and controlled Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) workflows. Special emphasis is placed on understanding the limitations of AI systems, validating outputs, and maintaining clear human accountability for decisions.

Throughout the program, students are taught that automation and AI are most effective when combined with strong operational discipline, sound engineering practices, and careful reasoning.

## Career Relevance

This program prepares students for roles where people are trusted with real systems and real business outcomes, including:

* Cloud Engineer
* Platform Engineer
* DevOps Engineer
* Site Reliability Engineer (SRE)
* Infrastructure Engineer
* Cloud Security Engineer
* Systems Engineer
* Automation Engineer

Graduates are trained to reason from evidence, manage risk, communicate technical decisions clearly, and operate systems responsibly. These skills are increasingly rare and highly valued across industries.

The program is designed for long-term employability and professional growth rather than short-term trends or technology hype.

## Operational Judgment

Technical knowledge alone is not enough.

A major focus of the program is operational judgment—the ability to understand context, evaluate trade-offs, identify risks, and make responsible decisions when systems, processes, or people are under stress.

Students learn that engineering is not merely about deploying technology. It is about understanding consequences, maintaining reliability, and protecting the systems that organizations depend on.

This emphasis on judgment, accountability, and disciplined execution distinguishes the program from traditional training approaches.

## Who This Program Is For

Applied Cloud & Platform Engineering is for serious students who are willing to work consistently over time and accept temporary discomfort in exchange for real capability.

A college degree is not required. Discipline, curiosity, persistence, and patience matter more than background.

This program is not for those seeking shortcuts, quick results, or passive learning. It is for those who want durable skills, practical competence, and the ability to contribute meaningfully in complex technical environments.

Students who complete the program successfully will possess not only technical knowledge, but the confidence and judgment required to work with systems that matter.
